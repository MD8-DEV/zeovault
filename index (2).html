Abstract

With the current demands for better sustainability and better well-being, Egypt has faced several grand challenges preventing reaching these goals, with major ones being dealing with arid areas and pollution and its consequences. The purpose of this study is to design a water purification system in a rural area with limited access to water, Bahr El-Batts area, and investigate how a feedback system can be implemented to better manage the water purification cycles.

The proposed solution is to use a system of three layers: Zeolite, Electrocoagulation, and UV sterilization to treat salinity, turbidity, and bacteria concentration respectively, and to utilize relays and sensors to facilitate the movement of the water. The layers were put into three containers, all connected to a central container that houses the wastewater, moving the water to each layer to filter its respective parameter.

The design requirements that the prototype was tested for were to decrease the TDS to below 500ppm, the TVOCs to 2000ppb as an indicator for bacteria, and the turbidity to 5% turbidness. The major findings after gathering the results were that the TDS reached 105ppm±10ppm, the TVOCs reached 1980ppb±297ppb, and the turbidity reached 5%±0.5%, leading to the certainty that the prototype is working efficiently, and that the problem to be solved was manageable.

System Efficiency

The energy consumed per liter by the prototype over the 5 cycles is found to be 30,879 Joules/liter.

The total produced clean water is 2 liters over the span of 1 hour.

Analysis Highlights

Zeolite Layer

Zeolite is a porous aluminosilicate material with a three-dimensional structure that functions through adsorption processes. Its skeletal structure with channels and chambers gives it an excellent ability to adsorb contaminants.

Key mechanisms:

  • Ion exchange: Captures positive cations like Na+ and K+
  • Physical adsorption: Removes solid pollutants through multilayer adsorption

Electrocoagulation Layer

Electrocoagulation utilizes electrolytic cells with a sacrificial iron anode that releases ions acting as coagulant agents. These ions neutralize particle charges and cause them to coagulate.

Removes pollutants through:

  • Floatation: Hydrogen microbubbles carry particles upward
  • Precipitation: Iron hydroxide forms a gelatinous precipitate that captures suspended particles

UV Sterilization Layer

The UV sterilization system uses short-wavelength UV-B light (254 nm) to kill or deactivate microorganisms by disrupting their DNA and RNA structure.

Advantages:

  • Environmentally friendly with no chemical by-products
  • Fast-acting: kills up to 99.99% of bacteria and pathogens within seconds
  • Achieved 81% efficiency in reducing bacteria indicators in our tests

Feedback System

Our automated feedback system consists of one main container connected to three treatment stages, with sensors monitoring water quality parameters.

The system:

  • Prioritizes TDS reduction before addressing turbidity and bacteria
  • Uses relays to control water pumps based on sensor readings
  • Optimizes energy consumption by only treating parameters that exceed threshold values
